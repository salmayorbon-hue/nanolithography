    <h2>Etymology</h2>
    <p>
        The word nanolithography is derived from Greek roots:

nano- (nanos) meaning dwarf or extremely small

litho- (lithos) meaning stone

graphy (graphia) meaning writing or patterning


Literally, it means “patterning at an extremely small scale."

    </p>

    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        Nanolithography is a set of fabrication techniques used to create and pattern structures at the nanometer scale (typically below 100 nanometers) on a material’s surface.
.
    </p>

    <h3>Denotation</h3>
    <p>
        The denotation is the literal, objective meaning:
    <ul>
        <li>A scientific and industrial process used to produce nanoscale patterns, especially in semiconductor and nanotechnology applications.
</li>
        <li</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="example">
        <strong>Example (Denotation):</strong><br>
        Nanolithography is used in semiconductor manufacturing to create microscopic circuits on computer chips.
    </div>

    <h3>Connotation</h3>
    <p>
        The connotation refers to the ideas and associations connected to the word:
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li>Advanced technology</li>
        <li>High-tech research and industry</li>
        <li>Precision and innovation</li>
        <li>Cutting-edge science</li>


    </ul>
